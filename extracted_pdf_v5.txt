Abstract—Identifying skin illnesses rapidly and correctly is
a significant hurdle in dermatology, primarily due to delayed
appointments with specialists. Meanwhile, the rapid growth
of machine learning, especially Convolutional Neural Networks
(CNNs), has shown tremendous success in analyzing medical
visuals. However, transforming these theoretical network models
into practical, user-friendly forms for everyday people remains
challenging. This study bridges that gap by introducing a smart
diagnostic system embedded in an interactive chatbot. Utilizing a
fine-tuned EfficientNetB0 architecture, our application analyzes
macroscopic photos uploaded by the user while simultaneously
asking about symptoms, such as itching or bleeding, to generate
a comprehensive risk profile. We trained the classifier on a
dataset of over 15,390 clinical images covering 11 different skin
conditions, yielding reliable diagnostic performance. The chatbot
guides users through the process step-by-step, explaining the most
probable condition and advising on the next medical actions.
Ultimately, this approach reduces the friction of preliminary
health screenings and provides an easily scalable solution for
modern telemedicine systems.

I. INTRODUCTION
A. Introduction and Motivation
The skin functions as the outer barrier of the body, making
it susceptible to a vast array of conditions. Certain issues are
relatively harmless, such as minor rashes or acne, whereas
others can be invasive and potentially fatal, notably melanoma.
Recent epidemiological data highlights that dermatological
issues are among the most frequent health complaints world-
wide. Early detection can drastically alter a patient’s outcome;
for instance, the prompt removal of a melanoma significantly
improves survival rates. Nevertheless, many individuals face
difficulties seeing a dermatologist promptly. Consultations can
be expensive, experts are often fully booked, and rural regions
frequently lack local specialized care.
In recent years, deep learning has introduced powerful new
methods to address this problem. By analyzing detailed visual
textures in photographs, computer vision algorithms can now
identify indicators of skin diseases with accuracy approaching
that of human clinicians. The primary motivation for this
project is the desire to make preliminary skin examinations
universally accessible. By coupling a robust machine learning
classifier with a simple internet-based chatbot, we intend to
dismantle the obstacles that stop people from seeking early
assessments, offering a convenient and reliable means for
anyone to evaluate their symptoms from home.
B. Problem Statement
Currently, both patients and healthcare providers encounter
several major challenges when striving for the early identifi-
cation of skin issues:
• Prolonged Wait Times: Scheduling a visit with a der-
matologist typically involves weeks or months of waiting.
In that period, aggressive skin diseases can progress and
metastasize.
• Basic Online Checkers: The majority of symptom
checkers available online rely purely on the text answers a
user provides. They do not evaluate pictures of the actual
lesion, which frequently leads to incorrect assessments
and undue anxiety.
• Complex Medical Tools: Although excellent AI diag-
nostic instruments exist, they are generally designed for
hospital settings. They are complicated to operate and not
meant for a layperson checking a mole at home.
• Ignoring Symptom History: Numerous image-focused
AI applications only analyze the photo. They fail to ask
how long the lesion has been present, or if it causes
pain or bleeding, thereby missing a critical piece of the
diagnostic puzzle.
C. Project Objective
The central objective of our project is to create an accurate
and intuitive application that combines photographic analysis
with standard symptom screening. We integrated this com-
bined methodology into a simple chat interface that extensively
simplifies preliminary health evaluations.
We concentrated on the following primary milestones to
achieve this:
• AI Image Model: We trained an EfficientNetB0 network
to correctly identify eleven distinct skin conditions, en-
suring the model remains fast while retaining its capacity
to recognize crucial shapes and colors.

• Smooth User Interface: We engineered a stable server-

• Joint Risk Evaluation: We formulated logic that com-

II. REQUIREMENT ANALYSIS
A. Feasibility Study
Prior to coding, we evaluated whether building and main-
taining this system was a realistic endeavor.
• Technical Feasibility: The technical strategy to utilize
TensorFlow, Python, and Flask is highly pragmatic. Be-
cause we selected EfficientNetB0, the model is compact
and does not necessitate massive servers or costly GPUs
to execute standard predictions, maintaining quick re-
sponse times.
• Financial Feasibility: Employing open-source software
like Keras and Flask ensures there are no prohibitive
licensing fees. The web application is relatively inex-
pensive to host, making it highly economical for health
clinics aiming to expand their virtual services.
• Operational Feasibility: Our structure is tailored for
average users. Because the tool resembles a standard
texting interface, individuals inherently know how to
interact with it. They require no tutorial, rendering it
exceptionally user-friendly.
B. Existing System Analysis
Drawbacks of Current Systems: Numerous online health
utilities feel akin to completing a lengthy form. They ask
excessive questions simultaneously and fail to guide the user
empathetically. If they do accept image uploads, they routinely
ignore textual descriptions entirely. Behind the scenes, older
online tools sometimes utilize outdated network architec-
tures like VGG, which are sluggish and consume substantial
memory. Furthermore, they typically present the user with a
confusing array of percentages without clarifying what those
statistics actually imply for their well-being.
Our Solution: The telehealth landscape lacks a straightfor-
ward web tool that marries high-fidelity computer vision with
clear chatbot profiling. By addressing both aspects in a single
application, our proposed system bridges this gap securely and
intuitively.
C. Requirements Elicitation
Whether the individual utilizing the app is a concerned
parent or a general practitioner seeking a second opinion, the
interface must be pristine. The system must seamlessly accept
standard photos taken on mobile phones under varying light
conditions and swiftly return comprehensible feedback.
D. Requirement Specification
1) Functional Requirements:
• Information Ingestion: The application must consume
file uploads flawlessly and also process the yes/no re-
sponses submitted in the chat. Upon photo upload, the
backend must autonomously crop and resize it so the
neural network can interpret it.
• Generating Predictions: Following image preparation,
the server must pass it through the trained model to
determine which of the 11 skin conditions it most likely
indicates. The model must compute which condition
holds the highest probability.
• Providing Clear Feedback: The system must deliver a
response directly into the chat window. It should inform
the user of the suspected disease and include a distinct
warning label, such as ”moderate risk,” to direct them
appropriately.
2) Non-Functional Requirements:
• Speed: The system must feel analogous to a real conver-
sation. From the instant the user clicks ”upload,” the final
verdict must return swiftly to prevent user frustration.
• Scalability: We must employ stateless web routing. In
this manner, if the application receives unusually high
traffic during a specific period, it can effortlessly provi-
sion additional servers to manage the load.
• Simplicity: The user must never encounter code excep-
tions, loading tensor graphs, or file format errors. All
intense computational tasks must occur entirely hidden
from view.
3) Development & Run Time Requirements:
• Software Stack: Our foundation is Python 3, Tensor-
Flow, Flask for managing incoming internet connections,
alongside fundamental HTML and CSS to style the chat
window on the client side.
• Hardware Setup: The server hosting the application
requires sufficient memory to keep the AI model actively
loaded at all times to avoid latency. It must manage
standard CPU requests smoothly.
III. DESIGN
A. System Architecture
Our project is modularized into three distinct layers, ensur-
ing all processes execute cleanly without intersecting errors,
as illustrated in Fig. 1.
This architecture delegates operations effectively:
• The Client Layer: This constitutes the web page the user
views. It contains the chat window and scripts that quietly
transmit text and images to the server without refreshing
the browser.
• The Web Server Layer: Developed with Flask, this
central layer receives the user’s incoming image, inspects
it for security parameters, temporarily saves it, and con-
ditions it for evaluation.
• The AI Layer: Within this layer, our pre-trained model
remains operational in active memory. It accepts the

B. Module Breakdown of Proposed System
• Image Formatting Subsystem: This module constrains
every uploaded photo to a standardized dimension and
normalizes color scales. Consequently, shadows or bright
flashes from a mobile camera do not disorient the AI.
• Classification Engine: This component analyzes the
standardized photo and matches it against thousands of
learned patterns. By leveraging EfficientNetB0, it scruti-
nizes relevant textures on the skin rather than ignoring
them or being misled by typical background scenery.
• Risk Assessor: This algorithm evaluates the conclusions
from the classification engine in conjunction with the
user’s text inputs. For instance, if the software assesses
the lesion as a minor problem but the user reports chronic
bleeding, the risk assessor elevates the severity level.
C. System Deployment Design
Individuals access a web link to commence their diagnostic
evaluation. Concurrently, our web server retains the model
in active memory constantly. This bypasses the need for the
server to load a massive file every time an image is uploaded,
which would drastically impede performance. Due to this
configuration, the user experiences exceptionally low-latency
interactions.
IV. IMPLEMENTATION
A. Technology & Tools
• Python Language: Python serves as our core program-
ming language due to its seamless integration of web
development libraries and scientific computing toolkits.
• TensorFlow and Keras: These frameworks supplied
the infrastructure we utilized to construct, train, and
archive the AI model. They manage the complex gradient
computations invisibly.
• Flask: A minimalist framework for organizing internet
traffic. It enables us to establish the necessary endpoints
for receiving images with minimal complexity.
• Matplotlib: We deployed this visualization tool during
the development phase to generate graphs confirming our
AI model’s learning progress across epochs.
B. Implementation of Core Modules
Data Gathering and Training: We assembled a substantial
dataset comprising over 15,390 images illustrating various
skin anomalies. To prevent the AI from merely memorizing
specific photographs, we implemented data augmentation. We
rotated, zoomed, and mirrored the images during training. This
trained the model to identify skin diseases regardless of camera
orientation. Furthermore, because we possessed thousands of
images for common diseases and significantly fewer for severe
conditions, we applied class weighting. This compelled the
algorithm to focus intensely on rare diseases, ensuring they
were not overlooked.

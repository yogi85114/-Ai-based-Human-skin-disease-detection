Frequently Asked Questions
How should I interpret Turnitin's AI writing percentage and false positives?
The percentage shown in the AI writing report is the amount of qualifying text within the submission that Turnitin’s AI writing 
detection model determines was either likely AI-generated text from a large-language model or likely AI-generated text that was 
likely revised using an AI paraphrase tool or word spinner.
 
False positives (incorrectly flagging human-written text as AI-generated) are a possibility in AI models.
 
AI detection scores under 20%, which we do not surface in new reports, have a higher likelihood of false positives. To reduce the 
likelihood of misinterpretation, no score or highlights are attributed and are indicated with an asterisk in the report (*%).
 
The AI writing percentage should not be the sole basis to determine whether misconduct has occurred. The reviewer/instructor 
should use the percentage as a means to start a formative conversation with their student and/or use it to examine the submitted 
assignment in accordance with their school's policies.
What does 'qualifying text' mean?
Our model only processes qualifying text in the form of long-form writing. Long-form writing means individual sentences contained in paragraphs that make up a 
longer piece of written work, such as an essay, a dissertation, or an article, etc. Qualifying text that has been determined to be likely AI-generated will be 
highlighted in cyan in the submission, and likely AI-generated and then likely AI-paraphrased will be highlighted purple.
 
Non-qualifying text, such as bullet points, annotated bibliographies, etc., will not be processed and can create disparity between the submission highlights and the 
percentage shown.

Abstract—Detecting skin-related ailments swiftly and accu-
rately represents a persistent barrier within the field of der-
matological medicine, primarily limited by the lack of prompt
access to specialized medical professionals. In the wake of rapid
developments in the domain of machine learning, intricate CNN
architectures have demonstrated extraordinary proficiency in
classifying diagnostic visual data. Nevertheless, converting these
complex algorithms into accessible, user-centric applications for
the general public proves to be a substantial hurdle. The
present research tackles this specific limitation by innovating
a smart diagnostic sorting mechanism naturally integrated into
a responsive chatbot interface. Propelled by a meticulously
calibrated EfficientNetB0 computational model, the suggested
architecture evaluates user-uploaded macroscopic photographs
simultaneously with actively collected symptomatic indicators
(e.g., pruritus severity or localized hemorrhage) in order to
perform a systematic, layered risk categorization. Trained uti-
lizing a heterogeneous dataset containing more than 15,390
clinical images covering 11 unique structural categories, the
neural system exhibits substantial diagnostic dependability. The
embedded digital companion navigates the individual through-
out the diagnostic process, translating the evaluated condition
into specific danger levels and recommending following medical
steps. Such a comprehensive computing structure significantly
diminishes the difficulty associated with early health checks while
operating as a highly expandable component within modern
remote medical networks.
Index Terms—Artificial Intelligence, Pathological Classifica-
tion, Deep Learning architectures, Neural Network Chatbots,
Telehealth, WSGI frameworks.
I. INTRODUCTION
A. Introduction and Motivation
Acting as the primary exterior shield of the human physique,
the dermal layer often displays irregularities varying from
common non-threatening abnormalities, such as acneiform
eruptions, to highly malignant and potentially lethal cancer-
ous formations, chiefly malignant melanoma. Epidemiological
statistics reveal that skin-related disorders rank among the
most universally widespread health issues. Swift identification
combined with clinical treatment critically determines the
healing path of the patient; accelerating the surgical removal
of melanomas fundamentally saves lives. Nevertheless, ob-
taining quick professional assessment is often obstructed by
prohibitive medical costs, a severe shortage of qualified skin
specialists, and immense spatial distances isolating individuals
from sophisticated healthcare centers.
Lately achieved breakthroughs in computational algorithms,
particularly the widespread adoption of complex deep learning
structures, offer a uniquely powerful tool for mechanically
performing early triage. By recognizing complex, microscopic
structural patterns inside visible sores—features routinely
overlooked by non-expert eyes—artificial vision systems are
able to achieve accuracy comparable to specialists. The pri-
mary motivation for this project stems from the communal
need to distribute and make skin checks universally available.
By incorporating cutting-edge categorization engines within an
easy-to-use internet-connected chat platform, this study aims
to tear down the traditional walls connected to preliminary
medical examinations, providing immediate, trustworthy, and
ubiquitous diagnostic approximations to society at large.
B. Problem Statement
People and medical associations consistently face ingrained
obstacles when striving to pinpoint and triage epidermal ab-
normalities:
• Delayed Clinical Access: Securing a professional derma-
tological consultation routinely involves extended wait-
ing periods. During this latency, aggressive pathological
states possess the potential to rapidly advance or metas-
tasize.
• Suboptimal Self-Evaluation Platforms: Current on-
line diagnostic surveys lean heavily on subjective user-
provided textual descriptions. These mechanisms fun-
damentally lack the anatomical precision provided by
direct pixel-based visual assessment, frequently resulting
in erroneous conclusions and unnecessary distress.
• Exclusive and Convoluted Tooling: The majority of ex-
isting diagnostic AI utilities are engineered strictly for in-
tegration within hospital networks and clinical pipelines.
These systems sport dense, non-accessible user interfaces,
rendering them practically useless for an anxious patient
seeking immediate, independent triage.
• Unimodal Blind Spots: Analyzing a purely static pho-
tograph neglects vital temporal and sensory data (e.g.,

C. Project Objective
The primary goal of this undertaking is to construct a
cohesive, extremely accurate computational tool that simulta-
neously digests pathological visual inputs alongside pertinent,
user-supplied symptomatic responses. This fused data pipeline
is navigated through a user-friendly, natural-language conver-

This primary ambition is realized through the systematic
execution of these pivotal milestones:
• Advanced Model Embeddedness: Modifying and train-
ing an EfficientNetB0 computational graph to supply
robust classification metrics across eleven heterogeneous
dermatological categories, leveraging transferred feature
hierarchies to optimize pattern recognition.
• Elastic Interface Conception: Constructing an agile and
fault-tolerant software backend utilizing Python and the

browser protocols to orchestrate seamless user interaction

a coherent, multi-tiered risk stratification (e.g., nominal,
moderate, severe).
II. REQUIREMENT ANALYSIS
A. Feasibility Study
A comprehensive viability assessment was performed to
gauge the practical success rate, structural constraints, and
realistic deployment potential of this framework.
• Technological Viability: The endeavor is fundamentally
sound. It harnesses extensively validated libraries such
as TensorFlow, the Keras API, and Flask. The strate-
gic choice of the EfficientNetB0 topology guarantees a
minimized parameter footprint while yielding exceptional
accuracy. This facilitates rapid forward-propagation on
standard web-server processors, sidestepping the financial
necessity for dedicated GPU clustering during standard
consumer queries.
• Financial Viability: The architecture proposes a spectac-
ular fiscal advantage for modern telehealth providers. By
building entirely upon open-source software distributions,
capital expenditure on software licensing is practically
eradicated. Furthermore, automating the initial patient
sorting phase drastically trims administrative overhead,
permitting the system to accommodate massive concur-
rent traffic volumes with minimal fiscal scaling.
• Operational Viability: The user experience is metic-
ulously optimized for frictionless public adoption. By
masking the dense mathematical complexities of deep
learning behind a familiar chat interface, the platform
eradicates operational friction. Consequently, zero train-
ing or technical literacy is required from the end-user,
ensuring immediate usability.
B. Existing System Analysis
Shortcomings of Current Implementations: Existing dig-
ital symptom checkers overwhelmingly lack conversational
empathy and guided user flow. Standard dermatological appli-
cations frequently serve as rudimentary upload forms devoid
of interactive dialogue. Moreover, they struggle immensely
to synthesize pixel data with subjective qualitative metrics
like pain intensity. Computationally, legacy tools continue
to rely on antiquated topologies (e.g., VGG-series networks)
which impose heavy computational lag and memory bloat.
Additionally, generic applications merely output raw statistical
probabilities, failing to translate these numeric vectors into
actionable, layman-comprehensible health directives.
The Addressed Void: The principal deficiency within the
contemporary telemedicine landscape is the stark absence of a
unified, highly accessible web-portal that organically merges
a lightweight, high-fidelity computer vision engine with a
deterministic symptom-gathering chat protocol. Our proposed
architecture cleanly resolves this discrepancy.

analytics, risk tiers, and navigation cues in an aesthetically
pleasing manner.

A. System Architecture

structural blueprint, ensuring a strict boundary between op-

• Client Presentation Layer: An interactive, browser-

• Middleware Control Layer: Flask-powered applica-

than accepting the pure unadulterated network output

strategy that scales the diagnostic severity index up or
down gracefully, guided exclusively by explicit text trig-
gers generated during the symptom interrogation session.
